How to be happy at mid-life

 

First let me start off with Im no Doctor Phil, Life Coach or anything more than just a person who has always had a pretty good life but, felt like there was something missing a lot of the time.

I felt happy most of the time but true, however, long-term happiness, eluded me. Some may call this depression, but for me, it wasnt. And, it wasnt that there was always something greater and better that I was chasing either. I know about watering my own grass.

I wasnt looking to escape myself and I certainly never wanted to be anyone but me. Sure I could be prettier, smarter and have nicer stuff but really, does any of that make someone happy for very long?

Pretty fades, smarter becomes irrelevant, and you cant take stuff with you.

But, I always had this little hole that I couldnt fill. I got some therapy; I had my share of boyfriends to fill it and, Ive spent my way through pretty good sums of money only to end up right back to where I had always been. Back to me the person with the hole.

So when I turned fifty life clicked for me. I finally got it. I was happy all along but, I was creating my unhappiness by the disappointment that I created about, all of the ways that I thought life should be.

The truth is, life is half chance and half results.

Blame it on the media, magazines, reality shows, parents or whatever but, how to be happy comes back to whether we are comfortable in our skin, at any stage in life. Some are luckier than others and get this sooner than I did at fifty but, never the less, I got it before it was too late. Better late than never.

That was it. I was never comfortable in my skin. I figured at age fifty; if I wasnt comfortable by now, Id better figure out how to get that way or figure out an exit plan because I certainly wasnt going to allow myself to grow old and bitter.

Moreover, I didnt want to walk around thinking a part of me was missing for the next half of my life.

It doesnt matter what age you are, how to be happy is about being comfortable in your skin. Its not about comparing yourself to anything other than the person that you were yesterday. Not tomorrow because, you dont have that day yet and, you may never get it.

Another thing that has changed me is the acceptance that life is beautiful and, its quick and, those that we love are here one day and gone the next. Like both my mother and my brother.

Let me say that when you forget that your mom has passed away but, you pick up the phone to call her and then you realize that she is no longer there, not today, not tomorrow, not ever again, life becomes pretty sobering.

If you are fortunate to have not lost a parent, please, call them every day because, when you do, youll feel a kind of pain the hurts beyond what you thought was even possible (sorry. I know you don’t need me to tell you that).

When we realize and contemplate that we too only have a short amount of time, we dont have much time other than to be happy. It is our duty to ourselves to give ourselves this gift. This is what most people will remember you for. Not stuff, not your looks, not how much money you had. This is your true legacy.

I know that really bad things happen in life and, these things will pass. We will all die and so will the people we love. We will have financial difficulties, and life will sometimes punch us so hard that it knocks the wind out and sometimes for longer than we had planned. But, life goes on with or without us, and thats just how it is. This is the acceptance part that we must do.

Im not here to give you a cheesy line about how grateful you should be. Or that what you have others would probably kill to have. I will say that you and I should take some time every day to realize how short life is and understand that, we dont have a lot of time other than to live our lives to the fullest while we are here for the short time that we have.

There is no magic to being happy. The secret is to accept the beautiful life that God has given, dont over think, add a little gratitude and, keep smiling knowing that you have this day which may be your last.

Its the voice that we have always wanted to hear but didnt take its advice until now. Its time for a change and with change comes healing and transformation.

The key to making a change is wanting to do it. Even if we fail on our journey, the more we keep trying, the higher the chance is that we will succeed. I dont need a diet to deprive myself. As soon as I say diet, I get a negative emotion that pulls me in the opposite direction, and I automatically go right back to the way I was or sometimes worse.

Sometimes instead of feeling good, I feel bad, and this is what I focus on. I realize that I dont need to live like this regularly. The fact is, I dont need to diet; I need to eat better. Once I fall, I know why I fell, and I dont fall that way again.

No sense in kicking ourselves when we are down. It doesnt make sense to be defeated before we start. I have wasted a lot of time in my life, not by trying and failing, nope, I have lost more time not doing anything at all.

At home thyroid test

 

At home test below:

 

Its believed by many doctors that our basal metabolic temperature or BMT is the most accurate measure of thyroid function.

We can check our own BMT at home by using an oral thermometer. Not a digital one.

 

This is how you do it:

Note: Please do not use this as medical advice. Please see your doctor for a proper diagnosis.

  1. When you are ready to go to bed, shake an oral thermometer down and put it next to your bed where you can easily reach it in the morning. Dont shake it down in the morning because this can raise your body temperature and the test wont be accurate.
  2. When you wake up in the morning, put the thermometer in your armpit and leave it there for 10 minutes. Try and lie as still as you can. Dont get up or move as this too will increase your metabolic rate thus, raising your temperature.
  3. After the 10 minutes are up, record your temperature.
  4. Repeat this process for 3 5 days (at least 3 but preferably 5).

If your BMT consistently falls below 97.8 F, you may have an underactive thyroid. BMT Normally ranges from 97.8 F to 98.2 F.

**If you have any kind of infection wait to take the test.

Where women go. The reality of middle-age

 

What are we waiting for? We cant wait for the future to happen and we certainly cant live in the past. In middle age and beyond, we have to come to terms with the fact that our choices have changed.

For some, the changes may not be good, for others, aging is a time when we can do the things we have only dreamed of.

I am inspired by Diana Nyad, the first women to ever swim to Florida from Cuba without a cage. This is extremely inspiring because she was a sixty-four-year-old woman!

I am impressed because I know a few sixty-year-olds who believe they cant park too far away from the grocery store doors because they dont want to walk that far, let alone, swim for fifty-three hours and one hundred and ten miles.

Did I mention this was her fifth attempt? Just goes to show you that we are never too old and to never stop trying no matter what. She made her first attempt when she was twenty-eight, and then in 2011, she tried three times at the age of sixty-two, and again at sixty-three.

When she finally accomplished the swim on September 2, 2013, she had three messages for us all: one, we should never, ever give up, two, youre never too old to chase your dreams, and three, it looks like a solitary sport, but it is a team.

Doesnt that sum up what we women are going through right now? Never give up on yourself, no matter what age you are. Enjoy your life, love yourself, and do what it is that feeds your soul. Its never too late to achieve and live your own personal dream. Just have the belief and do away with the fear.

Remember, large or small, your dream belongs to you, and its just as important as anyone elses. We are all living our own dream and we can create a new one if we dont like the one we are in.

Finally, you are never alone. It takes a team, meaning your friends, your family, your spouse, and your faith, especially faith in yourself. Whatever it is around you that lifts you and inspires you is what you are meant to be. It is important to always be mindful, moreover, keep an open mind.

You never know when the universe is offering you a gift, and if you are not awake, then you will miss opportunities and the energy will go elsewhere.

Too many times we get up, go to work, come home and then go to bed without taking even one second to appreciate the gift of life and what it has to offer us. For those of us who ask why are we here? The quick answer is to take a look around and embrace the gift of life.

The experience of being born may be the only reason we are here. We dont need to create chaos trying to make a bigger meaning than necessary, otherwise, we waste the time we have. Sure its great if you are able to leave a legacy, but even if you dont, its okay too.

The only wasted life is a life not lived fully. What does fully mean to you? If we embrace the experience of life without thinking there is an attachment, then we are living a life that is meant to be and, therefore, we receive the blessings that come with it.

The one day I will get there is now, and its a fork, and you must take the right path that makes you happy.

Our culture teaches us that we wont ever get there; therefore, we are in a constant state of searching and wondering whats next. I hope you realize your unique beauty and find the energy within right now, this moment. Every second counts.

This day too will pass and you will never get it back. Whatever holds you back now, you will look back in three or four years and realize how fantastic you really were. You will question why you stayed in certain states of mind, relationships, jobs or whatever held you back in the past.

As we get older, we tend to think we are more tired, especially women, because we are still thinking we can do it all. There are two kinds of energy. One is when you are tired and dont feel like doing anything. You may feel a bit down on yourself; you decide you dont have any energy to engage in life.

This energy level is the one when you havent slept or you need to eat, so you do and you get your energy back. The second energy level is when you have slept, eaten and still, you have no energy to enjoy life or to engage with others. Perhaps dare I say the word depressed? If you ate more or slept more, you would not find your energy in this state.

However, if suddenly your lottery ticket won or your soul mate came into your life in an instant, you would find your energy! This is the kind of energy you should strive to tap into. Wake up as though you have won the lottery of life, and you love yourself enough, more than anyone else could ever. You have the energy to live with excitement, just remove the negativity that is draining it.

We need to live in real moments in time. There is a book called Real Moments by Barbara De Angelis. If you take your life and your minutes for granted, then you should read this book. It will not only show you how to feel the sun on your face and the wind in your hair, it will show you how to live like you should before you die and actually feel like you live in the moment.

I know this may sound like a circular question, but there is a difference between going through the motions and living in the moment. When you actually live in the moment, time seems to stand still and you feel every experience and sensation happening in that moment. You owe it to yourself to live this way.

MAKING PEACE WITH AGING

 

Making peace with aging

Making peace with aging, Hmmm? How does a woman make peace with aging in a culture obsessed with youth?

It doesnt matter what age we are, the issue may change but the focus on aging remains the same. And that focus is how to keep a youthful appearance, stay healthy and remain active.

Its great for an aging woman to center around taking care of herself, but its another matter to obsess about getting old, and, yet, at some point we all do it. I guess you could say Im lucky because my appearance was never stunning, but I felt beautiful anyways.

I can age graciously by making peace with aging because I was, and still am, an ordinary woman, and thats okay. Its the beautiful women I feel for because they seem to be the people having the hardest time with aging.

I really believe when you are ordinary looking like myself, you age more lovely than when you have already reached your peak. However, all women get to the same place eventually, and there is no escaping it. But beauty is all relative anyway.

Sure, I look back at pictures and see my taught skin, my shapely body, my vibrant eyes and the youth that I didnt appreciate, and I am reminded again how fast life really goes by. With this reminder, I gain a moment of happiness because I also realize I am healthy, happy and look relatively good for my age.

I know life could be worse, so I dont dwell on the past or question what God has given me. It wont change where I am today or where I will be tomorrow. It would be wise to make peace with aging sooner than later just in case there is a little more work to do down the road.

Life is a creation in progress, and it shouldnt be that we are looking and waiting for the end, because that could come for anyone on any day. As we all know, there are no guarantees that we will have another day beyond this one.

I have heard it said that, if you dont feel old by the time you hit fifty, you soon will. If we live long enough, half of our lives will be spent as older women. I have never thought about it like this before. When I was young, I said I wanted to live forever!

Now, I just want to live a good life and be happy while I am here. I dont think the answer to getting old is lying about our age, either. Some women add a few years to their age when asked so that others might say they look good for their age.

How can this make us feel good because we, if no one else, still know how old we really are? Dont fall into the trap of being afraid to tell your age. Just making it another year is a miracle at any age, young or old

 

Free yourself to let grow old compassionately

 

 

Grow old with compassion for yourself

 

Call it growing old, getting old, aging, or whatever you want to call the inevitable. Getting old is going to happen no matter what. As we move towards the later years and age, there is something we still have yet to do and that is to forgive.

Forgive ourselves and forgive others, to let go and free ourselves to grow old with compassion for ourselves. Maybe we could have been better wives, mothers, sisters, givers, or whatever you may have regrets about, so what, the past is the past and it no longer matters anymore.

Whats done is done, now its about being kind to yourself. If you need to let others know what you now realize, then let them know and ask for forgiveness, forgive them if you need to and move on.

Whatever we have been searching for outside of ourselves is already within us. There is nothing to find out there that we dont already have inside. I talk to or hear about many older people that are deeply sad about the past. Maybe its things they wished they had done or wished they hadnt done.

If you are holding in sadness, despair, regret or any other emotion that you may be attributing to unhappiness, then just let it go. If you can do something about it, do it now and do it quickly, but dont dwell on it. Whatever the past was, it doesnt equal the future and the future is now. There is no reason not to forgive ourselves for whatever mistakes we have made in the past even if they were made only by omission.

In order to grow old, we must not fear growing old. The biggest problem that we all have, both men and women, is fear. We are afraid to live, to be alive, and to risk being ourselves.

Nothing defines us like death, and yet death is not our greatest fear, but genuinely living is the real fear. We have all had bad luck, bad breaks, heartbreak, or things didnt turn out the way we wanted them, however, we and the world still keep going.

We have to get old. We have no choice, but we continue to hold in pain and, with every breath, we dont let it out. We have to let go of pain and release it along with the blame and guilt. If you cant do this, then you owe it to yourself, and to those who love you, to make peace with yourself so that you can fully live and love those around you.
